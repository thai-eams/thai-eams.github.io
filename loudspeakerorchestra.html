TEAMS and its sound systems

As a traveling organization and a group of artists who are also technicians and technologists, we are neither bound by school-specific thinking nor institutional constraints. Not being bound to any institution, however, mainly presents us with one problem: we do not own any space that we can build an acousmonium and simply leave it there. From the start, we have used this to our advantage as we could experiment with different arrangements in different spaces.

Below I will be recounting all the sound systems we have performed and worked on, along with discussing what each system offers in place of one another.

Conclusion

Every system we have discussed so far here is really based on the spontaneous spirit of being Thai, or rather the Thai-ness of "we use whatever we find". As someone who has been with all the iterations of the TEAMSpace, these arrangements of loudspeakers has somehow found its own special place in my heart without me knowing it. I really hope this feeling continues to only grow stronger as we go forward as a TEAM.

Before closing off, I really want to give my personal thanks and dedication to my Bangkok Kunsthalle partner and collaborator Mark Chearavanont for always helping TEAMS and providing us with opportunity to build our system at the Bangkok Kunsthalle. I also want to give my humblest gratitudes to everyone who has helped with building every iterations of the TEAMSpace, including my mentors Thanapat Ogaslert and Scott Wilson, my best friend Nattakon Lertwattanaruk, and TEAMS's friend Dirk Johan Stromberg. Every piece of inspiration, ideas, and thoughts that have led me to interpret the French acousmonium as the TEAMSpace I owe to the lineage of electroacoustic music, the technologists, the scholars, the artists, and everyone who keeps pushing for a better future in sound.

- Sippapas Thienwiwat
