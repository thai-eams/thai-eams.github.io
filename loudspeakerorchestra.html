TEAMS and its sound systems

August 2026 version

As a traveling organization and a group of artists who are also technicians and technologists, we are neither bound by school-specific thinking nor institutional constraints. Not being bound to any institution, however, mainly presents us with one problem: we do not own any space that we can build an acousmonium and simply leave it there. From the start, we have used this to our advantage as we could experiment with different arrangements in different spaces. This also means that we have to make do with whatever is present at the venue, thus leading us to adapting our technical advisor Thanapat Ryan Ogaslert's Behringer X32 Producer and TEAMSmulch, a Pure Data-based sound diffusion script, as the build blocks for our system. Our loudspeaker orchestra takes a lot from the BEAST system in Birmingham, from philosophy to loudspeaker arrangements, but with a lot of Thai twist. There is this certain phrase in Thai, "ไทยประดิษฐ์", literally meaning "Thai invention" which is a phrase used to describe the Thai spirit of invention, of making things our own, of putting a Thai twist on something, and in Thai legalese it also means a self-made unregistered firearm.

Below I will be recounting all the sound systems we have performed and worked on, along with discussing what each system offers in place of one another.


CCC Quad Diffuse (8.2)

The earliest iteration of the TEAMSpace saga can be traced back to the 'evals' event at the Bangkok Kunsthalle, back when Cornea Cochlear Club was still very active compared to now. It all started back when I just came back from Birmingham (my pilgrimage to BEAST FEaST 2025), and Ryan contacted me about this gallery that has 8 Genelecs.

This would later become the first step towards making TEAMS what it is now. 'evals' also marked the first time I met Yves Pokakunkanon (msyves), who would later become our video art curator and now our general manager.


TEAMSpace 8 @ TICF 2025, College of Music, Mahidol University

Back in 2025 I was still very much involved in the activities of the Mahidol University-based Triphens Ensemble as its Technical Director. In April 2025, while I was retreating quietly to Hong Kong during the Songkran holidays, Triphens then-director Tanawat Naruepai rang me about curating a mini-concert for TICF2025, to which I said I will be writing something for, along with performing my piece Tec(h)tonic (2024-2025) with Laphatrada Wananukul.

The piece that I promised him I would write would later turn out to be "khûu / คู่", a commission from PGVIM and BEAST for PGVIS 2025: BEASTs of Wonderland (which I diffused the piece there on their 20.2 system 1 week after TICF). Thus, I used this opportunity to build a BEAST Main 8-styled array in the MACM hall.

TEAMSpace 26.2

TEAMSpace 26.2 was conceived for TagTEAMS 2026, Thailand's first festival of electroacoustic music, acousmatic music, and digital arts. I want to make a strong claim to being the first as there has been a lot of words from members of the extended sound art and 'experimental music' community in Thailand had been diluting the term 'electroacoustic' a lot and mentioned that it is not the first time for 'electroacoustic' festival. Well, love or hate me, it is.

TEAMSlide 32

I think it is impossible to talk about TagTEAMS 2026 without mentioning our customized fader surface, TEAMSlide 32. I commissioned TEAMS's resident tinkerer Tanatorn Pravalpadd to make a 32-fader surface for sound diffusion back in January 2026, and after some time this turned out as the final product. Now when I say 'surface for sound diffusion', I mean it literally. What is special about the TEAMSlide is that the fader caps were based on a molding clay model that were molded to my finger shape, thus giving it a shape that locks the fingertip onto the fader cap seamlessly.

Conclusion

Every system we have discussed so far here is really based on the spontaneous spirit of being Thai, or rather the Thai-ness of "we use whatever we find". As someone who has been with all the iterations of the TEAMSpace, these arrangements of loudspeakers has somehow found its own special place in my heart without me knowing it. I really hope this feeling continues to only grow stronger as we go forward as a TEAM.

Before closing off, I really want to give my personal thanks and dedication to my Bangkok Kunsthalle partner and collaborator Mark Chearavanont for always helping TEAMS and providing us with opportunity to build our system at the Bangkok Kunsthalle. I also want to give my humblest gratitudes to everyone who has helped with building every iterations of the TEAMSpace, including my mentors Thanapat Ogaslert and Scott Wilson, my best friend Nattakon Lertwattanaruk, and TEAMS's friend Dirk Johan Stromberg. Every piece of inspiration, ideas, and thoughts that have led me to interpret the French acousmonium as the TEAMSpace I owe to the lineage of electroacoustic music, the technologists, the scholars, the artists, and everyone who keeps pushing for a better future in sound.

- Sippapas Thienwiwat
